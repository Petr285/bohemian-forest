Bohemian Forest Charts

Discover the Bohemian Forest through data and statistics. From visitor numbers to wildlife observations, these charts provide a glimpse into the dynamic natural life of Šumava. Data are based on long-term monitoring by the Šumava National Park administration and environmental researchers in 2024.

Monthly Visitors (2024)

Visitor numbers peak during the summer months of July and August, when hiking and cycling are most popular. Winter months attract fewer tourists but offer peaceful scenery and excellent conditions for cross-country skiing.

Average Monthly Temperatures (°C)

The Bohemian Forest has a cool mountain climate. Temperatures rarely exceed 17°C in summer and often drop below freezing in winter — creating the misty, moody atmosphere the region is famous for.

Wildlife Sightings (2024)

Red deer are the most frequently observed large mammals in Šumava. The rare Eurasian lynx, symbol of the park, has been successfully reintroduced and sightings are slowly increasing thanks to protection efforts.

Forest Composition

Spruce remains the dominant tree species in Šumava, a result of historical forestry practices. However, reforestation programs are gradually restoring natural beech and mixed forests to improve biodiversity.
