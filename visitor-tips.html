Bohemian Forest Visitor Tips

  • The best time to visit is May–October for hiking and cycling, or December–March for cross-country skiing and snowshoeing.
  • Modrava, Kvilda, and Srní are great starting points for exploring the central part of the park.
  • Don’t miss the Boubín Lookout Tower, the Černé and Čertovo Lakes, or the Vydra River valley with its dramatic rock formations.
  • Many trails are accessible and well-marked — the Světly vrch – Březník – Modrava route offers one of the best views of the wild Šumava landscape.
  • Visit the Information Centre in Kvilda or Srní to learn more about the local wildlife and conservation efforts.
  • In winter, you can experience quiet snowy forests and perfectly groomed ski tracks with almost no crowds.
  • Always be prepared for changing weather, especially in the higher parts — carry a rain jacket and warm clothes even in summer.
  • Respect nature: stay on marked paths and avoid disturbing animals, especially during dusk and dawn.
  • Some of the remote trails require a full day — bring snacks, water, and a map or GPS device.
  • Many areas in Šumava have no mobile signal, so inform someone about your route before heading out.
  • Biking is popular in summer; some trails are shared with hikers, so always stay alert.
  • Wildlife spotting: early morning or late evening increases chances to see deer, lynx, or even wild boar.
  • Photography tip: misty mornings create magical landscapes, especially around lakes and rivers.
  • There are several small guesthouses and mountain huts along hiking trails — check opening times in advance.
  • Camping is only allowed in designated areas — wild camping is prohibited to protect nature.
  • Public transport is limited — renting a car or bike can make exploring easier.
  • Cultural visits: explore historic villages with traditional wooden houses, or small museums about forestry and local traditions.
  • For a unique experience, visit the Peat Bogs Educational Trail to see rare plants and learn about their ecological importance.
